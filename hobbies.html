Hobbies

General

I often feel in this digital age that people only take a quick look at
your profile pic or home page and they figure they've got all the info they need.
If you've made it this far though, obviously you're not one of those people. So
here are some things that I enjoy:

Music

I started playing guitar when I was around 15. I played sports growing up but
this was the first time I felt I could truly excel and enjoy a hobby. Needless to say
that 15 years later, it's still pretty cool. I've worked with alot of
great people and although it's not my full time career, the people I've met
and the skills I've learned along the way make it worth while.



Coding

Code was something that was suggested to me from a friend who worked in music.
He kept saying, "Code is music. You have fundamentals, theory, and creativity,
you're just using a different keyboard." After some other friends of mine who were
also in music got killer new jobs in the tech field, I knew I could too. Hundreds
of dollars and hours of Udemy lessons later, I'm here to land a killer new job
as well.



Utah Jazz Basketball

I don't normally watch tv.. but when I do, it consists of me yelling at it
because the Jazz win in the regular season and blow our chances of
winning a championship in the playoffs. I know you are just as heartbroken
about this as I am.



Cars

The skills of maintenancing a car seems to be more and more scarce. There's
a gift that resides in being able to diagnose, cause/effect, critically think
your way through an issue and cars are great examples. My 08 Accord with 200k
miles on it maybe went to the shop twice and honestly wish I hadn't have sold it.
There's a certain gratification that comes when you piece back together all your
parts, you have oil/grease/blood all over your hands and shirt and the new strut
is no longer going to cause your tire to wear unevenly. Saving money is also cool
because who enjoys going to the dealership? (The answer is no one.)



Hot Wings

When I bought my house, my friend got me a Weber grill as a house warming gift.
Since then, I'd like to think I've mastered the charcoal wing technique. The trick is
to let your wings soak in milk for an hour prior to seasoning and marinating.
This helps the chicken retain the seasoning. Plus, you can't go wrong with that
sweet charcoal crunch.



Video Games

In the event that I'm not working, playing fetch with my dog, or sleeping,
it's nice to have some down time playing my Playstation 5. I don't play much
since I'm a grown up now, but it's definitely fun to saddle up my trusty steed
in Red Dead Revolver or lose all the time in Call of Duty because I don't have time
to be good at it anymore.



In Conclusion:

If you couldn't tell, I like to stay busy. Learning a new skill or hobby
is always a beneficial component of life so.. That's what I do.
